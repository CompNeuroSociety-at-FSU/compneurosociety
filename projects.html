This team is working on replicating this previously published computational neuroscience paper (https://www.eneuro.org/content/eneuro/6/2/ENEURO.0423-18.2019.full.pdf), which was coded in NEURON with Python. Our goal is to get published in ReScience! This would involve reproducing the results in a significantly different manner (could be in Python, but with a different package, or another language like Julia or Octave). Hosted with generous mentorship from Dr. Rodrigo Pena, Dr. Juan Lopez, and PhD student Ty Roachford from the Stiles Nicholson Brain Institute.

    Weekly Meetings:
  • 1-2 Collaboration sessions per month (virtual or in-person, will be 2 meetings per month if enough people vote to attend)
  • 2-3 Workshops per month led by Project Lead or Open Science Coordinators
  • Monthly check-in with the Pena Lab (virtual)

Open Science Workshop Coordinator

Compensation: $17.5-$22/hr (dependent on grant)

Commitment: 2-4.5 hrs/week (dependent on grant)

Duties: Conduct Open Science literature reviews; independently lead 2 or 8 one-hour Workshops over 2 or 7 months (supervised by Project Leads); coordinate with Technical Rigor Intern and other Open Science Coordinator(s) on pre/post-workshop assessments (primary responsibility for own workshops); assist Technical Assessment Coordinator with reproducibility documentation review. Report to Project Lead and Mentors.

Required: Communication, time management, literature review proficiency, professionalism, independent work/judgment, proficiency in presentation (PowerPoint/Google Slides) and document (Word/Google Docs) design, scheduling (Outlook/Google Calendar), Python, VS Code, scientific/academic writing.

Desired: Git/GitHub knowledge or self-learning, research documentation familiarity or self-learning.

Open Science and Technical Assessment Coordinator

Compensation: $17.5-$23.5/hr (dependent on grant)

Commitment: 2-4.5 hrs/week (dependent on grant)

Duties: Independently lead 2 or 5 technical Workshops (e.g., containerization, FAIR metadata, replication toolkits) over 2 or 7 months (supervised by Project Leads); coordinate with Technical Rigor Intern and other Open Science Coordinator(s) on pre/post-workshop assessments (primary responsibility for own workshops); review GitHub pull requests; address technical inquiries/support repository pushes.
